Framework for Evaluating and Documenting Topics in Clarity Faith

This framework serves as a guide for assessing, categorizing, and documenting topics within Clarity Faith’s wiki and communications. Its purpose is to ensure that stances are clear, logically grounded, and resonate with the faith’s principles of clarity, universal alignment, and intellectual integrity.

 

Core Principles

  1. Intrinsic Evaluation: Assess the essence of a concept based on its intrinsic qualities, independent of external misuses or associations.
  2. Separation of Concepts: Misuse or dissonance related to a topic is evaluated as a separate issue rather than conflated with the core concept.
  3. Clarity in Categorization: Every topic must fall primarily into one of three categories – Resonant, Dissonant, or Nuance – based on a majority evaluation of its nature and alignment with Clarity Faith’s values.
  4. Logical Transparency: Provide clear, rational explanations for why a topic is categorized as Resonant, Dissonant, or Nuance.

 

Categories of Evaluation

Resonant

  • Definition: Topics, practices, or concepts that align with the principles of clarity, authenticity, and universal resonance.
  • Example Criteria:
    • Fosters growth, healing, or understanding.
    • Enhances alignment with universal principles of clarity and harmony.
    • Non-destructive and non-manipulative in its essence.
  • Examples:
    • Homosexuality: Resonant as an authentic expression of love and identity.
    • Psychedelic Mushrooms: Resonant for their potential in spiritual and psychological healing.

Dissonant

  • Definition: Topics, practices, or concepts that misalign with Clarity Faith’s values by fostering harm, manipulation, or disharmony.
  • Example Criteria:
    • Exploits or manipulates individuals or systems.
    • Causes harm or promotes behaviors that diminish clarity and alignment.
  • Examples:
    • Promiscuity: Dissonant due to its tendency to prioritize impulsivity and diminish authentic connection.
    • Escapist drug use: Dissonant for fostering disconnection rather than clarity.

Nuance

  • Definition: Topics that possess elements of both resonance and dissonance, requiring detailed analysis to clarify their alignment.
  • Example Criteria:
    • Context-dependent or multifaceted issues where neither resonance nor dissonance overwhelmingly dominates.
  • Examples:
    • Transgenderism: The ideological framework of transgenderism is dissonant with Clarity Faith as it seeks to achieve concept removal and erode the clarity of innate biological realities. While the personal experiences of individuals may hold resonance in their pursuit of truth, the overarching ideology misaligns with the principles of clarity and universal alignment.

 

Methodology for Evaluation

Define the Concept:

  • Clearly state the topic under evaluation.
  • Identify its intrinsic qualities without conflating external associations or misuses.

Analyze Intrinsic Qualities:

  • Assess alignment with clarity, harmony, and universal principles.
  • Identify any inherent dissonance or misalignment.

Separate Misuse and Association:

  • Distinguish between the core concept and behaviors or narratives associated with misuse.
  • Document misuse as a universal issue if applicable.

Categorize with Justification:

  • Assign the topic to one of the three categories based on the evaluation.
  • Provide a rationale that explains why it predominantly aligns with the chosen category.

Document Supporting Arguments:

  • Use historical, cultural, scientific, and spiritual evidence to support the stance.
  • Highlight connections to Clarity Faith’s overarching mission and principles.

 

Example Applications

Homosexuality:

  • Category: Resonant
  • Justification: Intrinsically aligned with principles of love, authenticity, and clarity. Promiscuity, often associated with it, is recognized as a separate dissonant behavior not inherent to homosexuality itself.

Psychedelic Mushrooms:

  • Category: Resonant
  • Justification: Intrinsic qualities support spiritual and psychological clarity. Potential for misuse (escapism or harm) is a universal concern and not unique to mushrooms, making it a separate category.

Weed:

  • Category: Nuance
  • Justification: Medicinal and relaxing properties are resonant. However, cultural and recreational overuse leading to escapism introduces dissonance, requiring nuanced analysis.

 

Universal Misuse Framework

Certain behaviors or patterns of misuse apply universally across topics and should not dictate the evaluation of a concept’s intrinsic nature. These include:

  • Escapism
  • Harmful manipulation
  • Addictive behaviors

Each misuse is documented separately to clarify that it is not intrinsic to the evaluated topic but rather a universal behavioral issue.

 

Implementation for Documentation

  1. Establish Stance: Assign each topic to Resonant, Dissonant, or Nuance categories.
  2. Provide Explanations: Clearly articulate reasoning and supporting evidence.
  3. Separate Misuse Discussions: Create independent entries or sections for misuse categories that apply universally.
  4. Maintain Consistency: Apply the framework uniformly to all topics, ensuring clarity and integrity in Clarity Faith’s teachings.
